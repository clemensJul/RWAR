1. Target Customer Segments (Project Developers & Builders in the DACH Region)
We are focusing on the B2B real estate development sector, divided into three key tiers:

Corporate/Institutional Players (e.g., STRABAG Real Estate, UBM Development, CA Immo, Soravia): Developing massive districts and office complexes. They manage investments in the hundreds of millions and face the strictest ESG and EU Taxonomy requirements from financing banks.

Mid-Sized Regional Builders (e.g., SÜBA, Glorit, Buwog): Constantly acquiring new, often undeveloped plots or urban densification areas, frequently located near challenging topographies like slopes or rivers.

Specialized Project Developers: Firms revitalizing brownfields or old industrial sites where subsurface risks (groundwater, soil instability) are massive cost factors.

2. The Status Quo & Customer Pains
During the critical "Due Diligence" phase of plot acquisition, the current process is slow, expensive, and heavily fragmented :

Outdated 2D Authority Maps (e.g., eHORA, WISA, ZÜRS Geo): Developers rely on flat, 2D maps that only show historical data. They are difficult to integrate into modern CAD/planning tools and completely ignore future climate projections (IPCC scenarios).

Slow & Expensive External Consultants: Ordering geological or soil assessments takes weeks and costs thousands of euros. If a plot is ultimately deemed unsuitable, this money becomes a total "sunk cost."

Static ESG Data Providers (e.g., Munich Re): Existing tools deliver standard PDF reports with abstract scores (e.g., "Flood Risk: Medium"). They fail to visualize exactly where the water will pool, offering no spatial guidance for adjusting the building's placement.

3. RAWR's Solution & Unfair Advantage
RAWR solves these specific pains through an approach that cannot be easily copied or bought :

Speed & Cost-Efficiency (The "Pre-Check"): Instead of waiting weeks for a geological report, developers run the plot through RAWR and see potential 2050 hazards in minutes. This saves massive amounts of time and money during the critical purchasing phase.

From 2D Statics to 3D Dynamics: By simulating heavy rain runoff in 3D, developers can immediately adapt their designs (e.g., "Water pools in this sink, let's move the underground garage entrance"). RAWR is not just a risk tool; it is a direct planning enabler.

EU Taxonomy-Proofing for Banks: Developers must prove future viability to secure good credit conditions. RAWR's visual IPCC climate projections (Future-Trend) provide the perfect, undeniable argument for bank negotiations.

Actionable Advice over Panic: Rather than just flagging a high risk, RAWR provides immediate solutions (e.g., "High heavy rain risk detected: Budget €50,000 for retention areas and backwater valves"). This empowers developers to instantly recalculate project budgets or renegotiate the land purchase price.
